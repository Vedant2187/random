A

Algebra
A branch of mathematics that deals with symbols and the rules for manipulating those symbols to solve equations and analyze relationships.
Arithmetic
The branch of mathematics that deals with the properties and manipulation of numbers.
Absolute Value
The distance between a number and zero on a number line.
Angle
The figure formed by two rays sharing a common endpoint.

B

Binary
A number system that uses only two digits: 0 and 1.
Calculus
A branch of mathematics that deals with continuous change and motion.
Base
The number used as a repeated factor in a power or exponential expression.
Binomial
An algebraic expression with two terms, usually connected by a plus or minus sign.

C

Circle
A closed curve in which every point on the curve is equidistant from the center point.
Cosine
A trigonometric function that relates the adjacent side of a right triangle to its hypotenuse.
Congruent
Having the same shape and size.
Cylinder
A three-dimensional geometric shape with two circular bases connected by a curved surface.

D

Diameter
The distance across a circle, passing through its center point.
Derivative
A measure of how a function changes as its input changes.

E

Exponent
A small raised number that indicates how many times a base number should be multiplied by itself.
Equation
A mathematical statement that asserts the equality of two expressions.

F

Factorial
The product of all positive integers less than or equal to a given positive integer.
Function
A relation that associates each input value with exactly one output value.

G

Geometry
A branch of mathematics that deals with the properties, measurement, and relationships of points, lines, angles, surfaces, and solids.
Graph
A visual representation of data that shows the relationship between variables.

H

Hypotenuse
The side of a right triangle opposite the right angle.
Hyperbola
A conic section that consists of two disconnected curves, each of which is a mirror image of the other.

I

Integer
A whole number that can be positive, negative, or zero.
Infinity
A concept of a number that is larger than any finite number.

J

Justify
To provide reasoning or evidence to support a mathematical statement or conclusion.

K

Key
A piece of information used to encode or decode a message in cryptography.
Kilogram
The basic unit of mass in the International System of Units (SI).

L

Limit
A fundamental concept in calculus that describes the behavior of a function as the input approaches a certain value.
Logarithm
The inverse operation of exponentiation.

M

Matrix
A rectangular array of numbers, symbols, or expressions arranged in rows and columns.
Median
The middle value in a set of ordered data.

N

Natural Number
A positive integer (excluding zero).
Negative Number
A number less than zero.

O

Obtuse Angle
An angle greater than 90 degrees and less than 180 degrees.
Octagon
A polygon with eight sides.

P

Prime Number
A natural number greater than 1 that has no positive divisors other than 1 and itself.
Pythagorean Theorem
A fundamental relationship in Euclidean geometry that describes the relationship between the sides of a right triangle.

Q

Quadratic Equation
An equation of the form ax^2 + bx + c = 0, where a, b, and c are constants and x is the variable.

R

Radical
A symbol used to indicate a root of a number or expression.
Ratio
A comparison of two quantities expressed as a fraction.

S

Scalar
A quantity that is fully described by its magnitude or size.
Square Root
A value that, when multiplied by itself, gives the original number.

T

Tangent
A trigonometric function that relates the opposite side of a right triangle to its adjacent side.
Triangle
A polygon with three sides and three angles.

U

Unit
A standard quantity used to express measurement.
Undefined
A value or expression that does not have a meaning or cannot be determined.

V

Variable
A symbol used to represent an unknown value or quantity.
Volume
The amount of space occupied by a three-dimensional object.

W

Whole Number
A non-negative integer (including zero).

X

X-Axis
The horizontal number line in a coordinate system.

Y

Y-Axis
The vertical number line in a coordinate system.

Z

Zero
The number denoted by 0; neither positive nor negative.
